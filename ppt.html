The Anomaly Problem

We are dealing with a "one-in-a-thousand" event.

  • Extreme Skew: 492 fraud cases vs 284,315 normal transactions.
  • Cost of False Negatives: Direct financial theft and loss of assets.
  • Cost of False Positives: Customer churn due to blocked legitimate cards.

Key Insights

We identified that fraudulent patterns are tightly clustered in specific latent dimensions.

By isolating correlations, we prioritized features that show inverse relationships with the fraud class, ensuring the model focuses on high-signal components.

The "Grocery Store" Dilemma

A transaction comes in with a 20% fraud probability.

Traditional Default: 0.5 threshold says "Let it through."

Our Strategy: We adjust the threshold to 0.2. Why? Because the cost of missing a fraudster is 10x higher than apologizing for a false alarm.

Constraints & Technical Debt

Computation Limits

Significant GPU scarcity during the Deep Learning phase limited our ability to perform extensive hyperparameter tuning for the Autoencoder, impacting its baseline precision compared to XGBoost.

The "Black Box"

Because features are anonymized via PCA, the model lacks inherent interpretability. We can identify anomalies, but we cannot easily explain the underlying business reason for a declined transaction.

Wrap Up

High Precision. Low Friction.

We successfully built a hybrid system that catches known fraud patterns with 84% average precision while maintaining a scalable anomaly detection baseline.
